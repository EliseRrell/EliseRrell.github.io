Dizparc

March-May 2025

Background

The project originated from a need to design a platform for managing procurement projects, as existing solutions slowed down an already lengthy and resource-intensive process.

My Role

Responsibilities were shared among the team, and we employed various design methods to guide our decisions. I take particular pride in contributing to the interface’s color scheme and layout, as well as designing the logo (confidential).

Challenges

  1. Create an engaging and intuitive interface that conveys a calm colour choice while maintaining a unique appearance.
  2. Streamline document submission and approval processes.
  3. Limited access to respondents due to confidentiality.

Team structure

In a team of four, we collaborated to design the webpage, conduct user research, create wireframes and prototypes, and deliver a UX-focused solution for the client.

Project Management

Project description

Collaborated with Dizparc on a client-commissioned solution. Our team was tasked with interpreting client requirements and designing the solution from a UX perspective, while Dizparc’s developers created their own interpretation without UX designer support.

Design Process

User Research

We received support and materials from the client, which provided valuable domain knowledge. We then conducted a thorough netnography, comparing existing project management tools to inform our user research. During a workshop, participants shared their experiences and engaged in open discussions about what works and what doesn’t. With half of the participants attending remotely, we encountered challenges that became a valuable lesson: Miro proved difficult for first-time users, even those with general computer skills.

Wireframing and Prototyping

To prototype solutions, we combined UX expertise with insights gathered from research participants. After identifying requirements, we conducted brainwriting sessions where each team member interpreted them independently. We then evaluated the pros and cons of each idea from a UX perspective and used the strongest concepts to create low-fidelity wireframes.

Key Features

Task and workflow management

For the respondents, it was important to easily see which tasks needed to be completed, their deadlines, and who the information should be forwarded to, ensuring nothing was overlooked. By making information easily accessible, project efficiency could be optimized. This was supported through a visible, color-coded WBS that highlighted relevance, while completed tasks remained visible and were celebrated rather than disappearing.

Meeting scheduling and management

During the process, respondents emphasized the importance of saving documents, as it makes it easier to revisit and reuse previous work. In meeting management, it is therefore crucial that related documents cannot be deleted by just anyone, but only by a super-admin for security. Additionally, it is important in meetings to easily stay updated on expectations, ensuring no one leaves empty-handed and time is not wasted.

Project management

For project managers, it is important to have an overview of the project’s milestones, the sequence of activities, and who is responsible for each task. Since project managers often handle multiple projects, it is crucial to quickly get an overview of the different projects and their related documents.
